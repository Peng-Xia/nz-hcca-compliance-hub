# Volunteer Management Policy

> **[OFFICIAL — PRIMARY ENGLISH VERSION]** This is the legally operative version for official use. For the complete Chinese reference translation, see [`volunteer_management_policy_ZH_REF.md`](./volunteer_management_policy_ZH_REF.md). **In case of any discrepancy between the English and Chinese versions, this English version prevails.**

**NZ Chinese Heritage & Community Alliance Inc. (HCCA)**

---

## 1. Purpose / 目的

HCCA relies on volunteers to deliver its community programmes and events. This policy ensures that volunteers are recruited, managed, supported, and recognised in a fair, safe, and legally compliant manner.

HCCA依靠志愿者开展社区项目和活动。本规范确保志愿者的招募、管理、支持和认可以公平、安全且符合法律要求的方式进行。

---

## 2. Volunteer Principles / 志愿者基本原则

*(a)* **Voluntary:** Volunteering is given freely and without coercion.
*(b)* **Non-substitution:** Volunteers are not used to replace paid employees.
*(c)* **No financial gain:** Volunteers may not receive payment beyond approved expense reimbursements (consistent with the [Financial Control Policy](../finance_&_funding/financial_control_policy.md)).
*(d)* **Respect and inclusion:** All volunteers are treated with dignity and respect, free from discrimination.
*(e)* **Safety:** HCCA has a duty of care to volunteers under the Health and Safety at Work Act 2015.

---

## 3. Recruitment / 招募

3.1 Volunteers may be recruited through community outreach, word of mouth, or HCCA social media.

3.2 All prospective volunteers must:
- Complete a **Volunteer Registration Form** (Appendix A);
- Provide valid contact details;
- Confirm they have read and agree to the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md); and
- For roles involving children or vulnerable persons: provide consent for a **Police Vetting check** under the Oranga Tamariki Act 1989 / Vulnerable Children Act 2014.

---

## 4. Volunteer Roles and Briefings / 志愿者角色与情况介绍

4.1 Each volunteer must be assigned a clearly defined role before each event or activity.

4.2 All volunteers must receive a briefing before commencing their role, covering:
- Their specific duties;
- Health and safety hazards relevant to their role;
- Emergency procedures (evacuation, first aid location, emergency contacts);
- Privacy obligations (if they will have access to participant information); and
- Who to contact if they have concerns or questions.

---

## 5. Expenses / 费用报销

5.1 Volunteers are not paid for their time.

5.2 HCCA may reimburse volunteers for **actual, reasonable out-of-pocket expenses** incurred in the course of their volunteering, including:
- Travel costs (public transport or mileage at the IRD mileage rate);
- Meal costs (where volunteering extends beyond 4 hours, up to a set limit approved by the Committee); and
- Other pre-approved expenses.

5.3 Reimbursement claims must be submitted within 60 days with receipts and in accordance with the [Financial Control Policy](../finance_&_funding/financial_control_policy.md).

---

## 6. Health and Safety / 健康与安全

6.1 HCCA owes a duty of care to volunteers as workers under the Health and Safety at Work Act 2015.

6.2 HCCA will:
- Identify and manage hazards that volunteers may be exposed to;
- Ensure volunteers receive adequate information and training for their roles;
- Provide appropriate personal protective equipment (if required); and
- Not require volunteers to undertake tasks that are beyond their capability or that pose an unreasonable risk.

6.3 Volunteers must:
- Follow HCCA's health and safety instructions;
- Report any hazards, incidents, or near-misses to the event coordinator immediately; and
- Not place themselves or others at risk.

---

## 7. Privacy / 隐私

7.1 Personal information collected from volunteers is held in accordance with the Privacy Act 2020.

7.2 Volunteer information is used only for HCCA purposes (coordination, communication, safety) and is not shared with third parties without consent.

---

## 8. Recognition / 志愿者认可

8.1 HCCA values and recognises its volunteers. Recognition may include:
- Public acknowledgement at events and in communications;
- Volunteer appreciation events;
- Reference letters (upon request); and
- Other non-financial recognition as approved by the Committee.

---

## 9. Volunteer Conduct / 志愿者行为规范

9.1 All volunteers must comply with the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

9.2 Concerns about volunteer conduct should be raised with the event coordinator or, if serious, with the Committee.

9.3 HCCA may decline to engage or may discontinue the engagement of a volunteer whose conduct is inconsistent with HCCA's values, provided the volunteer is given a fair opportunity to respond to any concerns.
