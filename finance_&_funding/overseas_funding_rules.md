# Overseas Funding Compliance Rules

**NZ Chinese Heritage & Community Alliance Inc. (HCCA)**

> **Legislative Basis:**
> - Incorporated Societies Act 2022 — Subpart 2 (Financial Gain prohibition)
> - Charities Act 2005 — Financial reporting and charitable purpose requirements
> - Anti-Money Laundering and Countering Financing of Terrorism Act 2009 (AML/CFT Act) — applicable obligations for charities
> - Financial Markets Conduct Act 2013 (as applicable)

---

## 1. Purpose

HCCA may receive funding from overseas donors and sponsors in furtherance of its charitable purposes. This policy establishes the compliance framework for **receiving, recording, assessing, and reporting overseas funding** to ensure full transparency, prevent misuse, and protect HCCA's charitable status.

---

## 2. Definitions

**"Overseas Funding"** means any donation, grant, sponsorship, bequest, or other contribution received by HCCA from:
*(a)* a person or organisation based outside New Zealand; or
*(b)* a New Zealand-based entity that is substantially controlled by, or acts on behalf of, persons based outside New Zealand.

**"Overseas Donor"** means any person or entity providing Overseas Funding.

**"Overseas Funding Register"** means the register maintained under clause 5 of this policy.

---

## 3. Fundamental Prohibition on Financial Gain

### 3.1 Absolute Prohibition

Consistent with **Subpart 2** of the Incorporated Societies Act 2022 and HCCA's charitable registration:

> **NO overseas funding (or any other HCCA funds) may be paid, transferred, or otherwise distributed to any member, officer, volunteer, or person associated with them as personal financial gain.**

### 3.2 Permitted Payments

The only circumstances in which HCCA funds (including overseas funds) may be paid to individuals are:

| Permitted Payment | Conditions |
|------------------|-----------|
| Reimbursement of actual, documented expenses | Must be pre-approved; receipts required; recorded in accounts |
| Reasonable remuneration for services | Must be authorised by Committee; disclosed in Interests Register; commercial rate only |
| Interest on loans to HCCA | At a rate not exceeding the current commercial rate; documented loan agreement required |
| Rent for property leased to HCCA | At a commercial rate; documented lease agreement required |

### 3.3 Financial Isolation

Overseas funds must be received into and held in a dedicated HCCA bank account. They must **not** be commingled with any officer's or member's personal funds under any circumstances.

---

## 4. Pre-Receipt Due Diligence

Before accepting any overseas funding, the Treasurer (with Committee oversight) must complete the following due diligence:

### 4.1 Donor Identification

For **any** overseas donation, the following must be recorded:

- [ ] Full legal name of the donor
- [ ] Country of residence / place of incorporation
- [ ] Contact details (address, email, phone)
- [ ] Nature of the donor (individual / company / foundation / government agency / other)
- [ ] Relationship to HCCA or any HCCA member (if any)

### 4.2 AML/CFT Self-Assessment Checklist

For donations **exceeding NZD 1,500** (or equivalent) or cumulative overseas funding **exceeding NZD 5,000** in any 12-month period from a single source, the Treasurer must complete the AML/CFT Self-Assessment:

| Check | Result | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Is the donor from a FATF high-risk or non-cooperative jurisdiction? (see current FATF list) | ☐ Yes / ☐ No | If Yes: seek Committee approval and legal advice before accepting |
| Is the donor a Politically Exposed Person (PEP) or connected to a PEP? | ☐ Yes / ☐ No | If Yes: enhanced due diligence required; document basis for acceptance |
| Does the donation appear consistent with the donor's stated connection to HCCA's purposes? | ☐ Yes / ☐ No | If No: investigate and document |
| Are there any unusual features (e.g., unsolicited large donation, unusual payment method, request to pass funds to a third party)? | ☐ Yes / ☐ No | If Yes: treat as a red flag; do not accept without Committee resolution and legal advice |
| Have the donor's identity documents been sighted or verified? | ☐ Yes / ☐ No | Record verification method |
| Is the source of funds consistent with the donor's known profile? | ☐ Yes / ☐ No | If No: investigate before accepting |

### 4.3 Red Flags — Mandatory Refusal

HCCA must **refuse** overseas funding if:

*(a)* The identity of the donor cannot be verified;
*(b)* The donor is on a UN or NZ government sanctions list;
*(c)* There are reasonable grounds to suspect the funds are derived from unlawful activity;
*(d)* The donation comes with conditions that would compromise HCCA's independence or charitable purposes; or
*(e)* The Committee has not approved acceptance (for donations triggering enhanced review).

## 6. Reporting Requirements

### 6.1 Annual Report Disclosure

The HCCA Annual Report must include a section on overseas funding that discloses:

*(a)* The total amount of overseas funding received in the financial year;
*(b)* The number of overseas donors;
*(c)* For any single overseas donation exceeding **NZD 1,500**, the country of origin, the amount, and the purpose of the donation *(donor name may be withheld if the donor has requested confidentiality and the Committee approves)*;
*(d)* A statement confirming that AML/CFT self-assessments were completed for all reportable donations; and
*(e)* Confirmation that no overseas funds were distributed to members as personal financial gain.

### 6.2 Charities Services Reporting

All overseas funding must be reflected in the annual financial statements filed with Charities Services, in accordance with the applicable financial reporting tier.

### 6.3 Suspicious Activity

If at any point the Treasurer or any Committee member suspects that overseas funds received are connected to money laundering or the financing of terrorism, they must:

*(a)* Report the suspicion to the full Committee immediately;
*(b)* **Not deal with or return the funds** pending legal advice; and
*(c)* Consult with the Society's legal advisors about obligations under the AML/CFT Act and whether a Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) is required to be filed with the New Zealand Police Financial Intelligence Unit.

---

## 7. Financial Isolation Controls

To ensure overseas funds cannot be misused:

| Control | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Dedicated bank account | Overseas funds must be deposited into the HCCA general account (or a dedicated overseas funding sub-account if volumes warrant it) — never a personal account |
| Dual signatories | Any single payment from HCCA funds exceeding NZD 500 requires approval of two authorised signatories |
| Committee approval | Any expenditure of overseas funds exceeding NZD 2,000 requires a Committee resolution |
| Audit trail | All overseas fund movements must be traceable in the accounts ledger |
| No cash handling | Overseas funds must not be received or disbursed in cash without Committee resolution and receipt documentation |

---

## 8. Training and Awareness

8.1 All Treasurers and Committee members with financial responsibilities must be briefed on this policy upon taking office.

8.2 This policy must be reviewed at each AGM and whenever the AML/CFT Act obligations for charities are updated.

---

## 9. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually and whenever there is a change to applicable legislation, including updates to the FATF recommendations or changes to Charities Services guidelines on overseas funding.
