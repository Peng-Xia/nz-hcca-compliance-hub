# Financial Control Policy

For the complete Chinese reference translation, see [`financial_control_policy_ZH_REF.md`](./financial_control_policy_ZH_REF.md). **In case of any discrepancy between the English and Chinese versions, this English version prevails.**

**NZ Chinese Heritage & Community Alliance Inc. (HCCA)**

> **Legislative Basis:**
> - Incorporated Societies Act 2022 — Subpart 2 (Financial gain prohibition), Sections 51–61 (Officer duties)
> - Charities Act 2005 — Financial reporting obligations

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes HCCA's financial controls to ensure all funds are managed transparently, accountably, and in strict compliance with the prohibition on financial gain for members under the **Incorporated Societies Act 2022** and HCCA's charitable obligations under the **Charities Act 2005**.

---

## 2. Core Principle — No Financial Gain

> **HCCA is a not-for-profit society. It must not be conducted for the financial gain of any member, officer, or connected person. This obligation is absolute and non-negotiable.**
>
> *Incorporated Societies Act 2022, Subpart 2*

---

## 3. Bank Accounts

3.1 HCCA must maintain at least one **dedicated bank account** registered in the Society's name. No HCCA funds may be held in or transacted through any personal account.

3.2 All bank accounts must be disclosed to the Committee, and account signatories must be Committee-approved.

3.3 **Authorised Signatories:** The following positions are authorised signatories on HCCA bank accounts:
- Treasurer (primary)
- President (secondary)
- One additional Committee member as approved by the Committee

3.4 **Dual Signatory Requirement:**
- Payments **over NZD 500**: must be authorised by **two** signatories.
- Payments **NZD 200–500**: may be authorised by the Treasurer alone, but must be reported to the Committee at the next meeting.
- Payments **under NZD 200**: the Treasurer may authorise, record, and report quarterly.

---

## 4. Budgeting and Expenditure

4.1 An annual budget must be prepared by the Treasurer and approved by the Committee before the start of each financial year.

4.2 Any expenditure that **exceeds** a budgeted line item by **more than 20%** or **NZD 300** (whichever is greater) requires a Committee resolution before commitment.

4.3 **Event budgets** must be prepared and approved by the Committee before each HCCA event. Post-event financial summaries must be filed with the Treasurer within **30 days** of the event.

4.4 All expenditure must be supported by:
- An invoice or receipt;
- The name and purpose of the expense; and
- The authorising signatory's approval record.

---

## 5. Reimbursement of Expenses

5.1 Members, officers, and volunteers may be reimbursed for **actual expenses** incurred on behalf of HCCA, provided:

*(a)* The expense was pre-approved by the Committee or Treasurer (for amounts under NZD 200);
*(b)* Original receipts or equivalent documentation are provided;
*(c)* The reimbursement claim is submitted within **60 days** of the expense being incurred; and
*(d)* The expense is reasonable and directly related to HCCA's activities.

5.2 No reimbursement above **NZD 500** may be paid without a Committee resolution.

5.3 An officer may **not** approve their own reimbursement — a different authorised signatory must sign off.

---

## 6. Remuneration

6.1 HCCA officers and members serve in a **voluntary capacity** as a general rule.

6.2 Remuneration for specific services (e.g., a contracted event coordinator, accountant, or translator) is permitted only if:

*(a)* The remuneration is at a **commercial market rate** for the services provided;
*(b)* The arrangement is approved by Committee members who **do not have a conflict of interest** in the matter;
*(c)* The arrangement is disclosed in the **Interests Register**; and
*(d)* The remuneration is recorded in the HCCA accounts and Annual Report.

6.3 Remuneration arrangements involving an officer's family member or connected person require **full Committee approval** (the interested officer must recuse themselves) and must be disclosed.

---

## 7. Financial Records

7.1 The Treasurer must maintain accurate, up-to-date financial records, including:

- A cashbook or accounting ledger (cloud accounting software is recommended);
- Records of all receipts and payments;
- Bank reconciliations (monthly);
- An asset register for HCCA property; and
- The Overseas Funding Register (see [Overseas Funding Rules](overseas_funding_rules.md)).

7.2 Financial records must be retained for a minimum of **7 years**.

7.3 Financial records must be available for inspection by any Committee member and, upon reasonable notice, by members.

---

## 8. Financial Reporting

8.1 Annual financial statements must be prepared and presented to members at the AGM. The format must comply with the reporting tier applicable to HCCA under the Charities Act 2005 (determined by annual expenditure thresholds).

8.2 Financial statements must be filed with Charities Services as part of the Annual Return.

8.3 A Treasurer's Report must be presented at each Committee meeting.

---

## 9. Audit / Review

9.1 The Committee must determine annually whether the financial statements require an audit, a review engagement, or a compilation, in accordance with the Act and any Charities Services guidance.

9.2 If an audit or review is required, the auditor/reviewer must be independent and must not be a member of HCCA.

---

## 10. Fraud and Misappropriation

10.1 Any suspected misappropriation of HCCA funds must be reported immediately to the President (or, if the President is involved, to the Vice-President or Secretary).

10.2 The Committee must investigate all credible reports of financial misappropriation and may engage external legal or accounting assistance.

10.3 Confirmed misappropriation will be referred to the New Zealand Police and may be reported to the Registrar of Incorporated Societies and Charities Services.

---

## 11. Grants and Charitable Receipts

11.1 All grant applications must be approved by the Committee before submission.

11.2 Grants received must be applied strictly in accordance with the conditions set by the grant-maker. Any deviation requires written approval from the grant-maker.

11.3 Grant expenditure records must be kept in a format sufficient to satisfy grant reporting requirements.
