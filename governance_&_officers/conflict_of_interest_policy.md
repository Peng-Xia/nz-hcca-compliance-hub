# Conflict of Interest Policy

**NZ Chinese Heritage & Community Alliance Inc. (HCCA)**

> **Legislative Basis:** This policy gives effect to **Sections 62–73** of the Incorporated Societies Act 2022 and the general duties of officers under **Sections 51–61** of the Act.

---

## 1. Purpose / 目的

This policy ensures that officers and members of HCCA act in the best interests of the Society and avoid situations where their personal, financial, or other interests could conflict — or be perceived to conflict — with those of the Society.

本政策确保HCCA理事会成员及普通会员始终以组织最佳利益为行动准则，避免其个人、财务或其他利益与组织利益发生冲突或产生潜在冲突的情形。

---

## 2. Definitions / 定义

### 2.1 "Interest" / "利益"

For the purposes of this policy and consistent with **Section 62** of the Act, an officer has an **interest** in a matter if they:

*(a)* are a party to, or will or may derive a financial benefit from, the matter;
*(b)* have a financial interest in a person who is a party to, or will or may derive a financial benefit from, the matter;
*(c)* are a director, officer, member, or trustee of a person who is a party to the matter;
*(d)* are the spouse, civil union partner, de facto partner, child, or parent of a person who is a party to, or will or may derive a financial benefit from, the matter;
*(e)* are otherwise directly or indirectly interested in the matter; or
*(f)* a reasonable and objective person would consider could result in the officer failing to act in the Society's best interests.

### 2.2 "Conflict of Interest" / "利益冲突"

A **conflict of interest** arises whenever an officer's personal interests, or interests of persons connected to them, could improperly influence, or be perceived to improperly influence, their performance of their duties to HCCA.

### 2.3 "Interested Officer" / "有利益关系的职员"

An **interested officer** is any officer who has, or may have, an interest in a matter before the Committee.

---

## 3. Disclosure Requirements / 披露要求

### 3.1 Duty to Disclose *(Section 63 & 64)*

An officer who is **interested** in a matter must:

*(a)* **disclose the nature and extent of the interest** as soon as practicable after becoming aware of it;
*(b)* make the disclosure to:
  - the full Committee at a Committee meeting; or
  - the Chair/President, who must then bring it to the Committee's attention; and
*(c)* ensure the disclosure is recorded in the **Interests Register** (see clause 5 and the [Interests Register](interests_register.md)).

### 3.2 Timing of Disclosure / 披露时机

Disclosure must be made:
- **Before** any discussion or decision on the relevant matter; or
- As soon as the officer becomes aware of the interest, if it arises during the course of a meeting.

### 3.3 Ongoing Disclosure / 持续披露义务

Officers have a continuing obligation to update their disclosures. If an officer's interests change, they must make a further disclosure as soon as practicable.

---

## 4. Consequences of Disclosure — Restrictions on Interested Officers / 披露后的限制

### 4.1 Default Restrictions *(Section 65)*

After disclosing an interest, an interested officer **must not**, unless the Committee resolves otherwise:

*(a)* be present during any discussion of the matter by the Committee;
*(b)* vote on the matter; or
*(c)* sign any document relating to the matter on behalf of the Society.

### 4.2 Committee Resolution to Permit Participation *(Section 66)*

The Committee may, by resolution of the non-interested members, permit an interested officer to:

*(a)* be present and participate in the discussion;
*(b)* vote on the matter; and/or
*(c)* sign documents relating to the matter,

if the Committee is satisfied that permitting participation is in the **best interests of the Society** and not contrary to the Act.

Any such resolution must be recorded in both the Committee meeting minutes and the Interests Register.

### 4.3 Quorum / 法定人数

For the avoidance of doubt, an interested officer who is excluded from a vote does not count towards the quorum for that vote.

---

## 5. The Interests Register / 利益登记册

### 5.1 Maintenance of the Register *(Section 68)*

The Secretary must maintain an **Interests Register** in the form set out in [interests_register.md](interests_register.md). The register must be:

*(a)* kept at the Society's registered office or in another location approved by the Committee;
*(b)* available for inspection by any member upon request; and
*(c)* updated within **5 working days** of any disclosure being made.

### 5.2 Contents of the Register *(Section 68)*

The Interests Register must record:

*(a)* the officer's name and role;
*(b)* the nature and extent of the interest;
*(c)* the date on which the disclosure was made;
*(d)* any resolution permitting participation and the basis for it; and
*(e)* a summary of any action taken by the Committee in respect of the interest.

### 5.3 Annual Review

The Interests Register must be reviewed at each AGM. All serving officers must confirm their disclosures are current or update them as required.

---

## 6. General Member Conflicts of Interest / 普通会员的利益冲突

6.1 While the statutory conflict of interest obligations under Sections 62–73 apply to **officers**, ordinary members are also expected to act in good faith.

6.2 Any member (including non-officers) who has a material personal interest in a matter before a general meeting should:

*(a)* disclose that interest before the matter is discussed; and
*(b)* consider whether it is appropriate to abstain from voting on the matter.

---

## 7. Consequences of Non-Compliance / 违规后果

7.1 An officer who fails to disclose an interest as required may:

*(a)* have their vote or decision on the matter invalidated by the Committee or a court;
*(b)* be subject to disciplinary action by the Committee, up to and including removal from office;
*(c)* be liable under the Act for any breach of officer duties; and
*(d)* be referred for investigation to the Registrar of Incorporated Societies.

---

## 8. Examples of Conflicts of Interest / 利益冲突示例

The following are illustrative examples of situations that may give rise to a conflict of interest (this list is not exhaustive):

| Scenario | Likely Conflict | Recommended Action |
|----------|----------------|---------------------|
| Treasurer's spouse owns a venue being considered for an HCCA event | Financial interest via family member | Disclose, withdraw from discussion and vote |
| President is also director of a company applying for an HCCA grant | Direct financial interest | Disclose, withdraw from discussion and vote |
| Committee member is applying for a paid role within HCCA | Personal financial benefit | Disclose, withdraw from all relevant decisions |
| Officer receives an overseas donation from a relative | Personal/family connection | Disclose, ensure Committee approves handling |
| Vice-President has a personal dispute with a member subject to disciplinary action | Personal interest/bias | Disclose, recuse from all disciplinary proceedings |
