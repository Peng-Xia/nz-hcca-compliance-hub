**NZ Chinese Heritage & Community Alliance Inc. (HCCA)**

> **Legislative Basis:** Section 113, Incorporated Societies Act 2022

---

## 1. Statutory Requirement

Under **Section 113** of the Incorporated Societies Act 2022, every incorporated society **must** have a designated **Contact Person** at all times. The Contact Person acts as the primary point of contact between HCCA and the Registrar of Incorporated Societies.

---

## 2. Requirements for the Contact Person

The Contact Person must:

*(a)* Be an **individual** (not a body corporate);
*(b)* Be **18 years of age or older**;
*(c)* Be **ordinarily resident in New Zealand**;
*(d)* Have **consented in writing** to act as the Contact Person; and
*(e)* Have their **name and contact details** registered with the Registrar of Incorporated Societies.

---

## 3. Role and Responsibilities

The Contact Person's responsibilities include:

*(a)* Receiving official communications from the Registrar of Incorporated Societies on behalf of HCCA;
*(b)* Forwarding all statutory communications to the Committee without delay;
*(c)* Assisting the Society in meeting its annual reporting and notification obligations;
*(d)* Being available to respond to enquiries from the Registrar; and
*(e)* Maintaining current contact details with the Registrar.

**The Contact Person does not have governance authority by virtue of this role alone.** Their role is administrative and communication-focused. If the Contact Person is also an officer, their officer duties apply separately.

---

## 4. Appointment and Removal

4.1 The Contact Person is appointed by resolution of the Committee.

4.2 The Contact Person may be any individual who meets the requirements in clause 2, including a Committee member.

4.3 The Committee must notify the Registrar of Incorporated Societies of the appointment (or any change) within **20 working days**.

4.4 The Contact Person may resign from the role by giving written notice to the Committee. The Committee must appoint a replacement as soon as practicable to avoid any period where the Society is without a Contact Person.
