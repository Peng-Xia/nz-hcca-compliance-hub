## Part 1 — Name and Purposes *(Section 26(1)(a) & (b))*

### 1. Name

1.1 The name of the Society is **NZ Chinese Heritage & Community Alliance Incorporated**.

### 2. Purposes *(Section 26(1)(b))*

2.1 The Society is established exclusively for charitable purposes for the public benefit. The purposes of the Society are:

*(a)* **Cultural Heritage Preservation:** To preserve, celebrate, and promote Chinese cultural heritage, traditions, arts, language, and history among Chinese-heritage communities in New Zealand.

*(b)* **Cultural Integration:** To facilitate the integration of Chinese-heritage communities into New Zealand's multicultural society by fostering cross-cultural understanding, respect, and participation.

*(c)* **Community Support:** To provide social support, settlement assistance, and welfare services to Chinese-heritage residents, particularly newly arrived immigrants, elderly community members, and families.

*(d)* **Education and Youth:** To promote educational opportunities, leadership development, and cultural education programmes for young people of Chinese heritage in New Zealand.

*(e)* **Community Events:** To organise, support, and host cultural, educational, and social events that further the above purposes and benefit the wider New Zealand public.

*(f)* **Advocacy:** To advocate for the interests and welfare of Chinese-heritage communities in New Zealand's civic, policy, and cultural spaces.

2.2 The Society may do anything that is incidental or conducive to the attainment of the above purposes and that is consistent with operating as a registered charity under New Zealand law.

2.3 The Society's purposes are exclusively charitable within the meaning of the **Charities Act 2005**. The Society is not established for the financial gain of its members.

## Part 2 — Membership *(Section 26(1)(c) & (d) & (e))*

### 3. Membership Eligibility and Admission *(Section 26(1)(c))*

3.1 Membership of the Society is open to any individual who:

*(a)* is 16 years of age or older;
*(b)* supports the purposes of the Society;
*(c)* agrees to be bound by this Constitution and all policies of the Society; and
*(d)* has paid the applicable membership subscription (if any).

3.2 Applications for membership must be made in writing (including by electronic means) to the Secretary. The Committee may, in its absolute discretion, accept or decline any application for membership. The Committee is not required to give reasons for declining an application.

3.3 The Committee must maintain a register of members that includes the full name and contact details of each member, as required by the Act.

### 4. Membership Subscription *(Section 26(1)(d))*

4.1 The annual membership subscription (if any) shall be set by the Committee from time to time and notified to members at least 30 days before it becomes payable.

4.2 The Committee may waive or reduce the subscription for any member where it considers it appropriate to do so, including on grounds of financial hardship.

4.3 A member whose subscription is more than 60 days in arrears may, by resolution of the Committee, cease to be a member, provided the Committee has given that member at least 14 days' written notice of the proposed action.

### 5. Rights and Obligations of Members *(Section 26(1)(e))*

5.1 **Rights of Members:** Each financial member is entitled to:

*(a)* attend and vote at general meetings of the Society;
*(b)* stand for election to the Committee (subject to eligibility requirements in clause 11);
*(c)* receive notice of general meetings;
*(d)* inspect the register of members (subject to any privacy considerations); and
*(e)* receive a copy of the annual financial statements and annual report.

5.2 **Obligations of Members:** Each member agrees to:

*(a)* uphold the purposes, constitution, and policies of the Society;
*(b)* not act in a manner likely to bring the Society into disrepute;
*(c)* disclose any conflict of interest as required under the [Conflict of Interest Policy](../governance_&_officers/conflict_of_interest_policy.md); and
*(d)* pay membership subscriptions as set from time to time.

### 6. Cessation of Membership *(Section 26(1)(c))*

6.1 A person ceases to be a member of the Society if they:

*(a)* resign by giving written notice to the Secretary;
*(b)* die or (if a body corporate) are dissolved;
*(c)* are expelled under clause 7; or
*(d)* fail to pay their subscription and are removed under clause 4.3.

### 7. Expulsion of Members

7.1 The Society may expel a member if, after following the process set out in this clause and the [Dispute Resolution Policy](../dispute_resolution/dispute_resolution_policy.md), the Committee or a general meeting resolves that the member has:

*(a)* materially breached this constitution or any policy of the Society; or
*(b)* acted in a manner detrimental to the interests or reputation of the Society.

7.2 Before expelling a member, the Society must:

*(a)* give the member at least 21 days' written notice of the proposed expulsion, setting out the grounds;
*(b)* give the member a reasonable opportunity to respond in writing and, if they request, to appear before the Committee or a hearing panel;
*(c)* ensure the process is consistent with the principles of **Natural Justice** (the right to be heard, the right to an unbiased decision-maker); and
*(d)* follow the Schedule 2 safe harbour procedures (where applicable).

7.3 A member who is the subject of an expulsion decision may appeal that decision through the dispute resolution process in the [Dispute Resolution Policy](../dispute_resolution/dispute_resolution_policy.md).

## Part 3 — General Meetings *(Section 26(1)(f))*

### 8. Annual General Meeting (AGM)

8.1 The Society must hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) once in each calendar year within six (6) months after the end of the financial year.

8.2 The business at the AGM must include:

*(a)* receiving the annual report of the Committee;
*(b)* receiving and adopting the annual financial statements;
*(c)* electing or re-electing members of the Committee;
*(d)* appointing an auditor or reviewer (if required);
*(e)* setting the membership subscription for the coming year (if applicable); and
*(f)* any other general business.

### 9. Special General Meetings

9.1 A Special General Meeting (SGM) may be called:

*(a)* by the Committee at any time;
*(b)* by the President upon written request signed by not less than **10%** of financial members or **10 members**, whichever is greater; or
*(c)* by the Registrar of Incorporated Societies in accordance with the Act.

9.2 A SGM requested under clause 9.1(b) must be held within **28 days** of receipt of the written request.

### 10. Notice and Conduct of General Meetings

10.1 At least **14 days'** written notice (or **21 days'** notice for a meeting to consider a special resolution) must be given to all members before any general meeting.

10.2 Notice must set out the business to be transacted at the meeting.

10.3 **Quorum:** The quorum for a general meeting is **10 financial members** or **20% of financial members**, whichever is less, but no fewer than 5 members.

10.4 **Voting:** Unless a special resolution is required, decisions at general meetings are made by a simple majority of votes cast. Each financial member has one vote. The Chairperson has a casting vote in the event of a tie.

10.5 **Special Resolution:** A special resolution requires at least **75% of votes cast** by financial members present and voting. Special resolutions are required for constitutional amendments, dissolution, and any matter specified as requiring a special resolution in this constitution.

10.6 **Proxy Voting:** Members may appoint a proxy in writing to attend and vote on their behalf.

10.7 **Remote Participation:** The Committee may permit members to participate in general meetings by video conference or other electronic means, provided that all participants can communicate with each other in real time.

## Part 4 — The Committee *(Section 26(1)(g) & (h))*

### 11. Committee Composition and Officer Eligibility *(Section 26(1)(g) & Section 47)*

11.1 The Society is governed by a Committee consisting of the following officer positions:

*(a)* President
*(b)* Vice-President
*(c)* Secretary
*(d)* Treasurer
*(e)* At least two (2) and up to four (4) Ordinary Committee Members

11.2 **Eligibility — Section 47 Requirements:** A person is eligible to serve as an officer of the Society only if they:

*(a)* are 18 years of age or older;
*(b)* are a financial member of the Society;
*(c)* are not an **undischarged bankrupt**;
*(d)* have not been **prohibited** from acting as an officer under the Act or any other enactment;
*(e)* have not been **convicted of a crime involving dishonesty** within the past five years;
*(f)* are **not subject to a property order** under the Protection of Personal and Property Rights Act 1988;
*(g)* consent in writing to act as an officer; and
*(h)* are not otherwise disqualified under the Act.

11.3 If an officer ceases to meet any eligibility requirement, they must immediately notify the Committee and vacate their office.

### 12. Election and Term of Committee *(Section 26(1)(g))*

12.1 Officers are elected by financial members at the AGM by simple majority vote (or by special resolution where required).

12.2 The term of office for all officers is **two years**. Officers may be re-elected without limit.

12.3 If a vacancy arises on the Committee between AGMs, the Committee may co-opt a financial member to fill the vacancy until the next AGM, subject to the eligibility requirements in clause 11.2.

### 13. Removal of Officers *(Section 26(1)(g))*

13.1 An officer may be removed from office by a **special resolution** at a general meeting, provided that:

*(a)* the officer has been given at least **21 days'** written notice of the proposed resolution; and
*(b)* the officer has been given the opportunity to speak to the resolution at the meeting.

### 14. Powers and Duties of the Committee *(Section 26(1)(h))*

14.1 The Committee may exercise all powers of the Society that are not required by this constitution or the Act to be exercised by the members in general meeting.

14.2 The Committee's powers include (but are not limited to):

*(a)* managing the financial affairs of the Society and approving expenditure;
*(b)* entering into contracts on behalf of the Society;
*(c)* establishing subcommittees and delegating powers to them;
*(d)* making policies consistent with this constitution; and
*(e)* appointing a Contact Person under **Section 113** of the Act.

14.3 The Committee must:

*(a)* act in the best interests of the Society and in furtherance of its purposes;
*(b)* maintain the Interests Register required under **Sections 62–73** of the Act;
*(c)* ensure the Society does not operate for the financial gain of any member;
*(d)* provide members with access to financial information as required by the Act; and
*(e)* ensure the Society meets all obligations as a registered charity under the **Charities Act 2005**.

14.4 The Committee must meet at least **four (4) times per year**. A quorum at Committee meetings is a majority of the current Committee members.

## Part 5 — Amendment of Rules *(Section 26(1)(i))*

### 15. Amending This Constitution

15.1 This constitution may only be amended by a **special resolution** (75% majority) at a general meeting of the Society, provided that:

*(a)* at least **21 days'** written notice of the proposed amendment is given to all members;
*(b)* the full text of the proposed amendment is included in or attached to the notice; and
*(c)* the amendment does not contravene the Act or the Charities Act 2005.

15.2 Any amendment to the constitution must be notified to the Registrar of Incorporated Societies and to Charities Services within **20 working days** of the resolution being passed.

15.3 All proposed amendments must follow the [Compliance Git Flow](../GOVERNANCE.md) before being put to a vote.

## Part 6 — Dispute Resolution *(Section 26(1)(j))*

### 16. Dispute Resolution

16.1 Any dispute between members, or between a member and the Society, shall be resolved in accordance with the [Dispute Resolution Policy](../dispute_resolution/dispute_resolution_policy.md).

16.2 The dispute resolution process is consistent with the principles of **Natural Justice** as required by **Section 39** of the Act and incorporates the safe harbour provisions of **Schedule 2** of the Act.

16.3 No person who is a party to a dispute, or who has a material interest in its outcome, may participate as a decision-maker in that dispute.

## Part 7 — Financial Provisions *(Section 26(1)(k))*

### 17. No Financial Gain *(Section 26(1)(k) — Subpart 2)*

17.1 **The Society must not be carried on for the financial gain of any member.** This is an absolute prohibition.

17.2 No portion of the income or property of the Society shall be paid or transferred directly or indirectly to any member, officer, or person associated with them, except:

*(a)* in payment of **reasonable remuneration** for services actually rendered to the Society, provided the payment is authorised by the Committee and recorded in the minutes;
*(b)* in reimbursement of **actual expenses** properly incurred on behalf of the Society;
*(c)* as interest at a rate not exceeding the current commercial rate on money lent to the Society; or
*(d)* as rent at a commercial rate for property let to the Society.

17.3 Any payment under clause 17.2 must be:

*(a)* consistent with the [Financial Control Policy](../finance_&_funding/financial_control_policy.md);
*(b)* disclosed in the Interests Register; and
*(c)* approved by Committee members who do not have a conflict of interest in relation to the payment.

### 18. Financial Year

18.1 The financial year of the Society ends on **31 March** each year (or such other date as the Committee determines and notifies to the Registrar).

### 19. Financial Reporting

19.1 The Society must prepare annual financial statements in accordance with the Act and, if registered as a charity, in accordance with any tier requirements imposed by Charities Services under the **Charities Act 2005**.

19.2 Financial statements must be presented to members at the AGM. The Committee will determine, in accordance with the Act, whether an audit or review is required.

## Part 8 — Winding Up and Distribution of Assets *(Section 26(1)(l))*

### 20. Winding Up

20.1 The Society may be wound up by a **special resolution** (75% majority) at a general meeting called for that purpose, or by order of the court.

### 21. Distribution of Assets on Winding Up *(Section 26(1)(l))*

21.1 If the Society is wound up or dissolved, after paying all lawful debts and liabilities of the Society, **all remaining assets and property of the Society shall not be distributed to members** but shall be transferred to one or more organisations that:

*(a)* are registered as a **charity under the Charities Act 2005** or hold equivalent charitable status recognised under New Zealand law;
*(b)* are a **not-for-profit entity** lawfully operating in New Zealand;
*(c)* have purposes that are **consistent with or similar to** the charitable purposes of HCCA; and
*(d)* are determined by a special resolution of the members, or, in the absence of such resolution, by the Committee.

21.2 If no such organisation can be identified, the remaining assets shall be transferred to Charities Services or such body as directed by the High Court of New Zealand.

21.3 **No member, officer, or former member may benefit personally from any distribution of assets upon winding up.**

## Part 9 — Contact Person *(Section 113)*

### 22. Contact Person *(Section 113)*

22.1 The Society must, at all times, have a designated **Contact Person** as required by **Section 113** of the Act.

22.2 The Contact Person must be an individual who is 18 years of age or older, is ordinarily resident in New Zealand, and consents in writing to acting in that role.

22.3 The Committee shall appoint and maintain the Contact Person and ensure their details are current in the register held by the Registrar of Incorporated Societies.

22.4 Full details of the Contact Person role and responsibilities are set out in the [Contact Person Policy](../governance_&_officers/contact_person.md).

## Part 10 — General

### 23. Common Seal

23.1 The Society may, but is not required to, have a common seal. If the Society adopts a common seal, the conditions for its use shall be determined by the Committee.

### 24. Notices

24.1 Any notice required to be given under this constitution may be given in writing by post, email, or any other electronic means that provides a record of delivery.

### 25. Interpretation

25.1 In this constitution, unless the context otherwise requires:

- **"Act"** means the Incorporated Societies Act 2022.
- **"Committee"** means the governing body of the Society as constituted from time to time.
- **"Financial member"** means a member whose subscription is current (or who has been exempted from subscription by the Committee).
- **"Natural Justice"** means the principles of procedural fairness, including the right to be heard and the right to an unbiased decision-maker.
- **"Officer"** has the meaning given in the Act.
- **"Special resolution"** means a resolution passed by at least 75% of the votes cast by financial members present and voting at a general meeting of which proper notice has been given.
