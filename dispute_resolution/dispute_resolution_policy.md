# Dispute Resolution Policy

> **[OFFICIAL — PRIMARY ENGLISH VERSION]** This is the legally operative version for official submission, regulatory reporting, and governance purposes. For the complete Chinese reference translation, see [`dispute_resolution_policy_ZH_REF.md`](./dispute_resolution_policy_ZH_REF.md). **In case of any discrepancy between the English and Chinese versions, this English version prevails.**

**NZ Chinese Heritage & Community Alliance Inc. (HCCA)**

> **Legislative Basis:** Section 39 (Natural Justice) and Schedule 2 (Safe Harbour Dispute Resolution Provisions), Incorporated Societies Act 2022

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes a fair, accessible, and legally compliant process for resolving disputes and complaints within HCCA. It is designed to be consistent with the principles of **Natural Justice** as required by **Section 39** of the Incorporated Societies Act 2022 and incorporates the **safe harbour provisions** of **Schedule 2** of the Act.

---

## 2. Principles of Natural Justice

All dispute resolution processes within HCCA must adhere to the following fundamental principles of **Natural Justice**:

*(a)* **Right to be heard (Audi alteram partem):** Every person who is the subject of a complaint or proposed adverse decision must be given a fair opportunity to know the case against them and to respond, in writing or in person.

*(b)* **Rule against bias (Nemo iudex in causa sua):** No person who has a personal interest in the outcome of a dispute, or who is a party to the dispute, may act as a decision-maker in that dispute.

*(c)* **Timely process:** Disputes must be addressed in a timely manner, without unreasonable delay.

*(d)* **Transparency:** The basis for any decision must be communicated to the affected person(s).

*(e)* **Proportionality:** The outcome or sanction must be proportionate to the nature of the complaint or breach.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

*(a)* Disputes **between members** of HCCA;
*(b)* Disputes **between a member and the Society** (including its Committee or officers);
*(c)* **Complaints** by members about the conduct of other members or officers; and
*(d)* Matters relating to **proposed expulsion or other disciplinary action** against a member.

---

## 4. Step 1 — Informal Resolution

4.1 Where a dispute or complaint arises, the parties should first attempt to resolve it informally through direct, good-faith communication.

4.2 If the parties are unable to resolve the matter directly, either party may ask the **President** (or, if the President is a party to the dispute, the **Vice-President**) to facilitate an informal discussion.

4.3 If the matter is resolved informally, the outcome should be recorded in writing and signed by the parties. A copy should be filed with the Secretary.

4.4 Informal resolution should be attempted within **15 working days** of the dispute arising.

---

## 5. Step 2 — Formal Complaint to the Committee

5.1 If informal resolution is unsuccessful, a party may submit a **formal written complaint** to the Secretary.

5.2 The formal complaint must include:

*(a)* The name and contact details of the complainant;
*(b)* The name of the person(s) complained about (the "respondent");
*(c)* A clear description of the nature of the dispute or complaint;
*(d)* The dates and circumstances of the relevant events; and
*(e)* The outcome sought by the complainant.

5.3 The Secretary must, within **5 working days** of receiving a formal complaint:

*(a)* Acknowledge receipt of the complaint to the complainant;
*(b)* Provide a copy of the complaint (or a fair summary) to the respondent; and
*(c)* Notify the Committee.

5.4 The respondent must be given at least **10 working days** to provide a written response to the complaint before any decision is made.

---

## 6. Step 3 — Committee Hearing

6.1 The Committee must convene a hearing to consider the formal complaint within **20 working days** of receiving the respondent's response (or of the deadline for response passing).

6.2 **Composition of Hearing Panel:** The hearing must be conducted by Committee members who:

*(a)* Are not a party to the dispute;
*(b)* Have no personal interest in the outcome; and
*(c)* Have not prejudged the matter.

If insufficient unbiased Committee members are available, the Committee may appoint an independent person to serve on or chair the hearing panel.

6.3 **Procedure at Hearing:**

*(a)* Both the complainant and the respondent must be given the opportunity to present their case, in writing, in person, or both.
*(b)* Both parties may be supported by a support person of their choosing (but not a legal practitioner acting in a representative capacity, unless the Committee agrees).
*(c)* The hearing panel may ask questions of either party.
*(d)* The hearing may be conducted by video conference if agreed by the parties or if in-person attendance is not practicable.

6.4 **Decision:** The hearing panel must make its decision in writing within **10 working days** of the hearing concluding. The written decision must include:

*(a)* A summary of the complaint and the evidence considered;
*(b)* The findings of fact;
*(c)* The reasons for the decision; and
*(d)* The outcome, including any sanction or remedy imposed.

6.5 **Possible Outcomes:**

| Outcome | Applicable Where |
|---------|-----------------|
| No action required | Complaint not substantiated |
| Formal warning | Minor breach, first instance |
| Suspension from membership or office | Serious or repeated breach |
| Requirement to make apology or reparation | Interpersonal dispute |
| Expulsion from membership | Serious breach, as per Constitution clause 7 |
| Referral to relevant authority | Criminal conduct or regulatory breach |

---

## 7. Step 4 — Appeal

7.1 Either party may appeal the Committee's decision to a **general meeting** of the Society within **15 working days** of receiving the written decision.

7.2 The appeal notice must be in writing, addressed to the Secretary, and must state the grounds for the appeal.

7.3 The general meeting must be convened within **30 working days** of the appeal notice being received.

7.4 At the appeal meeting:

*(a)* A quorum must be present;
*(b)* The Committee members who made the original decision must not chair the meeting or vote on the appeal;
*(c)* Both parties must be given a fair opportunity to present their case; and
*(d)* The decision of the general meeting is final and binding on all parties.

---

## 8. Schedule 2 Safe Harbour Provisions

8.1 This policy is designed to take advantage of the safe harbour protections available under **Schedule 2** of the Incorporated Societies Act 2022. Compliance with the procedures in this policy constitutes compliance with Schedule 2.

8.2 The key Schedule 2 requirements reflected in this policy are:

*(a)* Written notice of the complaint provided to the respondent;
*(b)* Reasonable opportunity for the respondent to respond;
*(c)* Decision by unbiased decision-makers;
*(d)* Written reasons for the decision provided to affected parties; and
*(e)* An appeal mechanism available to affected parties.

---

## 9. Confidentiality

9.1 All parties to a dispute and all persons involved in the resolution process must keep the details of the complaint, the hearing, and the outcome confidential, except as required by law or as necessary to give effect to the outcome.

9.2 The Secretary must maintain a confidential register of complaints and their outcomes.

---

## 10. Anti-Victimisation

10.1 HCCA will not tolerate any victimisation, retaliation, or unfair treatment of a person who has made a good-faith complaint or participated in a dispute resolution process.

10.2 Any victimisation shall itself be treated as a serious breach and may result in disciplinary action.

---

## 11. Policy Review

This policy must be reviewed by the Committee at least every two years and whenever there is a significant change to the Act or relevant law.
